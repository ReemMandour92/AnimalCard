Seagull

They are migratory birds that migrate every winter, mostly from Siberia and the Mediterranean regions to the UAE shores, where they escape the cold to warm weather suitable for their habitat.

  • Scientific Name: Seagull
  • Average Length: smallest 120g(size 29cm) and largest 1.75 kg (size 75cm)
  • Average Lifespan: 10 to 15 years in the wild
  • Habitat: coastal regions, and near bodies of fresh water

Seagulls are seabirds with general colors of white or grey, often with black markings on the head and/or wings. They have a strong body, hooked bill, long legs with fully webbed feet. They are omnivorous and eat a wide variety of food, including fish, marine, and freshwater invertebrates. Seagulls can drink either freshwater or saltwater, though they prefer freshwater. They have supratropical glands(also called nasal or salt glands) which remove sodium chloride from the bloodstream.
